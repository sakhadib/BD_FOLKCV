Evaluation Scales and Rubrics (0–5)

All images are independently evaluated by expert annotators on five predefined scales. 
Each scale is scored on an integer range from 0 (lowest) to 5 (highest). 
Higher scores indicate stronger alignment with the evaluation criterion.

------------------------------------------------------------
1. Regional Cultural Authenticity
------------------------------------------------------------
Definition:
This scale measures the degree to which the image accurately represents the 
folk culture, lifestyle, and material elements of the specific Bangladeshi division 
being depicted.

Evaluation Criteria:
Annotators consider region-specific indicators such as traditional clothing, 
architectural forms, environmental context (e.g., rivers, hills, agriculture), 
tools, occupations, and culturally recognizable motifs associated with the target division.

Rubric:
0 – No recognizable or relevant regional cultural elements are present.
1 – Very weak or incorrect regional representation.
2 – Limited regional cues with noticeable inaccuracies or generic elements.
3 – Moderately accurate representation with some correct regional features.
4 – Strong regional authenticity with minor inaccuracies.
5 – Highly accurate, clear, and distinctive representation of the target division’s culture.

------------------------------------------------------------
2. Folk Art Stylistic Fidelity
------------------------------------------------------------
Definition:
This scale evaluates how closely the image adheres to traditional Bangladeshi folk 
art aesthetics rather than modern, photorealistic, or generic illustration styles.

Evaluation Criteria:
Annotators assess stylistic features such as symbolic or flattened perspective, 
decorative patterns, bold or non-naturalistic color usage, narrative composition, 
and visual traits characteristic of recognized folk art traditions.

Rubric:
0 – Entirely photorealistic or modern digital art style.
1 – Minimal folk-art influence.
2 – Partial folk stylization mixed with modern aesthetics.
3 – Clear folk-art inspiration with some modern deviations.
4 – Strong adherence to folk-art stylistic principles.
5 – Highly faithful representation of traditional folk-art style.

------------------------------------------------------------
3. Symbolic and Narrative Depth
------------------------------------------------------------
Definition:
This scale measures the extent to which the image conveys folkloric narratives, 
symbolic meaning, rituals, myths, or culturally embedded storytelling elements.

Evaluation Criteria:
Annotators look for visual storytelling, symbolic objects or figures, ritual scenes, 
mythological references, or implied narratives commonly found in folk traditions.

Rubric:
0 – No symbolic or narrative content.
1 – Very minimal or unclear symbolic meaning.
2 – Weak narrative or symbolic elements.
3 – Moderately clear symbolic or narrative content.
4 – Strong narrative or symbolic representation.
5 – Rich, layered, and culturally meaningful folkloric narrative.

------------------------------------------------------------
4. Visual Coherence and Artistic Composition
------------------------------------------------------------
Definition:
This scale assesses the overall visual quality, coherence, and compositional balance 
of the image, independent of cultural correctness.

Evaluation Criteria:
Annotators evaluate clarity of composition, color harmony, spatial consistency, 
object integrity, and the absence of visual artifacts or distortions.

Rubric:
0 – Visually incoherent, broken, or severely distorted.
1 – Poor composition with major visual issues.
2 – Noticeable compositional or visual flaws.
3 – Acceptable visual coherence with minor issues.
4 – Strong and well-balanced composition.
5 – Excellent visual clarity, balance, and artistic coherence.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. Emotional and Cultural Expressiveness
------------------------------------------------------------
Definition:
This scale evaluates how effectively the image conveys emotional tone, atmosphere, 
or lived cultural experience associated with folk life.

Evaluation Criteria:
Annotators consider emotional resonance, mood, facial expressions, gestures, 
environmental atmosphere, and the image’s ability to evoke cultural feeling.

Rubric:
0 – Emotionally flat or culturally disengaged.
1 – Very weak emotional or cultural expression.
2 – Limited emotional impact.
3 – Moderate emotional or cultural resonance.
4 – Strong emotional or cultural expressiveness.
5 – Powerful and immersive emotional or cultural impact.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. Hallucination
------------------------------------------------------------
Hallucinated or Non-Bangladeshi Elements:
Annotators indicate whether the image contains clearly identifiable elements that 
are culturally incorrect, foreign, or inconsistent with Bangladeshi folk traditions.

Values:
Yes / No
